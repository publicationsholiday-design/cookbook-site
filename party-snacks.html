Salted Caramel Popcorn Balls

Prep: 10 minutesCook: 10 minutesServes: 12 ballsBeginner

Ingredients

  • 10 cups plain popped popcorn (about 1/2 cup unpopped kernels)
  • 1/2 cup salted butter
  • 1 cup packed light brown sugar
  • 1/3 cup light corn syrup
  • 1 teaspoon sea salt, plus extra for sprinkling
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • Optional: 1 teaspoon vanilla extract, chopped toasted nuts, or mini chocolate chips for rolling

Instructions

  1. Spread the popcorn out on a rimmed baking sheet, removing any unpopped kernels.
  2. In a heavy-bottomed saucepan, combine butter, brown sugar, corn syrup, and salt. Cook over medium heat, stirring, until the mixture bubbles energetically and smells deeply caramelized, about 5–7 minutes.
  3. Remove pan from heat and stir in baking soda (and vanilla, if using)—the mixture will foam vigorously, creating lightness.
  4. Immediately pour hot caramel over popcorn. Working quickly, use a spatula to toss and coat each kernel.
  5. When cool enough to handle but still pliable (about 2–3 minutes later), grease your hands (or don gloves) and shape mixture firmly into balls about the size of a small apple—listen for satisfying crackles as caramel sets.
  6. If desired, roll balls in additional sea salt, nuts, or chocolate chips while slightly sticky. Set on parchment until fully cool and crisp.
  7. Work fast; caramel hardens quickly as it cools.
  8. Wrap individually in wax paper or cellophane for grab-and-go ease or gifting.
  9. Store in an airtight container at room temperature up to five days—they retain their chewy crunch beautifully.
  10. Serve as a whimsical centerpiece with cocktail picks for easy sharing.
Variations
  • Swap in vegan butter and agave nectar for a plant-based option.
  • Add a pinch of smoked salt or cayenne for an adventurous twist.

Chocolate-Dipped Pretzel Rods

Prep: 15 minutesCook: 5 minutes (chilling time: 30 minutes)Serves: 20 rodsBeginner

Ingredients

  • For Dipping:
  • 20 large pretzel rods
  • 10 oz semisweet or dark chocolate chips (milk chocolate or white chocolate may be substituted) For Finishing:
  • 1/4 cup assorted sprinkles, chopped nuts, freeze-dried berries, or flaky sea salt

Instructions

  1. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper for easy cleanup and nonstick surface.
  2. In a microwave-safe bowl, heat the chocolate chips in 30-second intervals, stirring after each, until melted and glossy. If using a double boiler, melt chocolate over barely simmering water while stirring until smooth. Avoid letting any steam or water touch the chocolate for best texture.
  3. Holding each pretzel rod, dip half to two-thirds of the rod into the melted chocolate, swirling gently to coat thickly—the chocolate should glisten and cling without dripping excessively.
  4. Immediately roll or sprinkle each dipped rod with your choice of toppings, pressing lightly so they adhere before the chocolate hardens.
  5. Lay decorated pretzels on the prepared baking sheet. Chill in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes, or until the chocolate has set firm with a satisfying snap.
  6. If chocolate begins to seize or thicken, add 1 teaspoon coconut oil to loosen and regain shine.
  7. Work swiftly if your kitchen is warm; refrigerate between batches to prevent melting.
  8. Store in an airtight container for up to one week. Arrange upright in glasses for a playful, grab-and-go presentation at parties.
Variations
  • Swap in gluten-free pretzel rods for guests with allergies.
  • For vegan options, use dairy-free chocolate and check labels on toppings.
