Grandma’s Holiday Apple Crisp Parfaits

Prep: 15 minutesCook: 25 minutes (for apple filling)Serves: 6 parfaitsMedium (great for family teamwork)

Ingredients

  • For the Apple Filling:
  • 3 medium apples (Honeycrisp or Gala), peeled and diced
  • 2 tablespoons pure maple syrup
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg
  • 1 teaspoon lemon juice
  • For the Crisp Topping:
  • 1 cup rolled oats (GF if needed)
  • 1/3 cup brown sugar (or coconut sugar)
  • 1/4 cup melted dairy-free margarine or butter
  • Pinch of salt
  • To Assemble:
  • 2 cups vanilla Greek yogurt (dairy-free or regular)
  • Festive sprinkles (gluten- and dye-free)

Instructions

  1. In a sauté pan, cook diced apples with maple syrup, cinnamon, nutmeg, and lemon juice over medium heat. Stir frequently until apples soften and syrup bubbles gently, about 10 minutes. Let the spicy-sweet aroma guide you—apples should be tender but not mushy.
  2. Preheat oven to 350°F. Combine oats, brown sugar, melted margarine, and salt in a bowl. Spread mixture on a baking sheet; bake until toasted and fragrant, about 8–10 minutes. Watch for a golden hue and listen for a faint crackle.
  3. To assemble parfaits, layer warm apple filling, dollops of yogurt, then crisp topping in glasses. Repeat layers, finishing with extra crisp and a sprinkle of festive decorations.
  4. Serve immediately for a cozy, comforting treat—or chill for a refreshing snack later.
Tips
  • Chop apples evenly to ensure uniform cooking.
  • Toast crisp topping close to serving time for ultimate crunch.
  • Store apple filling and crisp separately and assemble just before eating.
Variations
  • Use coconut yogurt for dairy-free parfaits.
  • Try pears instead of apples for a winter twist.
  • Add a dash of ginger for extra spice.

Date-Sweetened Cookies

Prep: 15 minutesCook: 12–15 minutesServes: 18 cookiesIntermediate

Ingredients

  • Dry ingredients:
  • 1 cup whole wheat flour (or certified gluten-free blend)
  • 1 cup old-fashioned rolled oats
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon Wet ingredients:
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter or coconut oil, softened
  • 1/4 cup unsweetened applesauce
  • 1 large egg (or flax egg: 1 tbsp flaxseed meal + 3 tbsp water)
  • 2 teaspoons pure vanilla extract Sweetener/add-ins:
  • 1 cup pitted dates, very finely chopped or pureed
  • 1/3 cup dark chocolate chips or chopped nuts (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons chia or sunflower seeds (optional)

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a bowl, whisk flour, oats, baking soda, and cinnamon until combined.
  3. Using another bowl, cream softened butter or coconut oil with applesauce using a spoon or mixer. Beat in egg (or flax egg) and vanilla until smooth and fluffy.
  4. Stir in chopped dates. The dough will be thick and speckled with caramel-colored flecks.
  5. Fold in dry ingredients gradually, mixing until just combined. If using, add chocolate chips or seeds.
  6. Drop tablespoonfuls of dough onto the prepared sheet, spacing about 2 inches apart. Flatten gently with your palm for even baking.
  7. Bake for 12–15 minutes, or until cookies turn golden at the edges and smell fragrant. They should be slightly chewy in the center and set on the bottom.
  8. Let cool on the baking sheet for 5 minutes, then transfer to a wire rack.
Tips
  • Soak dates in warm water for 10 minutes before chopping if they seem dry.
  • Don’t overbake; cookies will firm up as they cool.
  • Store in an airtight container for up to 4 days or freeze for longer storage.
Variations
  • Use a gluten-free flour blend to make cookies celiac-safe.
  • Omit chocolate/nuts for school-safe versions.
  • Amp up nutrition with grated carrot or zucchini added to the dough.
