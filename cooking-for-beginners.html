Base Grain Bowl Recipe

Prep: 15 minutes (plus grain cooking time if not using pre-cooked) Cooking Time: 20–40 minutes (for grains/roasting veggies) Skill Level: Beginner Servings: 1 (scale as needed for meal prep) Special Equipment Needed: Medium saucepan or rice cooker; sheet pan (for roasting); airtight containers

Ingredients

  • For the base:
  • ● 1 cup cooked grain (quinoa, brown rice, or farro—see substitutions)
  • For vegetables & proteins:
  • ● 1/2 cup shredded rotisserie chicken OR 1/2 cup cooked beans or baked tofu (chickpeas, black beans, or cubed tofu)
  • ● 1/2 cup cherry tomatoes, sliced
  • ● 1/2 cup Persian cucumber, sliced
  • ● 1/4 cup carrots, shredded
  • ● 1/4 avocado, sliced
  • For dressing and toppings:
  • ● 1 tablespoon vinaigrette dressing (lemon vinaigrette, tahini, or soy-sesame, adjust to taste)
  • ● 1 tablespoon crumbled feta cheese OR roasted seeds (pumpkin, sunflower) or chopped nuts (almonds, walnuts)
  • ● Salt and pepper to taste
  • ● Optional: fresh herbs like cilantro or parsley, pickled onions, or sauerkraut
  • Possible Substitutions:
  • ● Any whole grain: swap with white rice, millet, wheat berries, cauliflower rice for low-carb
  • ● Protein: use air-fried salmon, hard-boiled egg, tempeh, or leftover beef strips
  • ● Veggies: use roasted sweet potato, steamed broccoli, bell peppers, or whatever is on hand
  • ● Dairy-free: skip cheese or use a plant-based alternative

Instructions

  1. Cook your grains according to package instructions until tender but not mushy. For example, simmer quinoa in water until little tails appear and all water is absorbed. Fluff with a fork and let cool slightly before assembling your bowl.
  2. Prepare your protein. If using rotisserie chicken, shred with fingers or forks. Drain and rinse beans if using canned. For tofu, cut into cubes and bake at 400°F for 20–25 minutes until golden and firm.
  3. Prep vegetables: Slice cherry tomatoes and cucumbers, shred carrots, slice avocado just before serving to prevent browning.
  4. If roasting vegetables (optional): Toss veggies like sweet potato, cauliflower, or Brussels sprouts in olive oil, salt, and pepper; roast at 425°F for 20–30 minutes until edges are browned and they smell caramelized and sweet.
  5. Assemble your bowl:
  6. ○ Start with 1 cup of grains as your base (warm or room temperature).
  7. ○ Layer on protein and arrange vegetables around the bowl for color contrast.
  8. ○ Drizzle with dressing and add creamy or crunchy toppings (cheese, seeds, nuts).
  9. ○ Sprinkle with fresh herbs and a pinch of salt and pepper.
  10. ○ For extra crunch or tang, add pickled onions or a scoop of sauerkraut.
  11. Visual Cue: The bowl should look vibrant and inviting, filled with distinct textures and pops of color. You should smell fragrant herbs, roasted vegetables, and tangy dressing.
Tips
  • ● Common pitfalls: Avoid overdressing—add sauce right before serving for best texture, especially for meal prep. Keep delicate toppings (avocado, nuts) in a separate container if prepping ahead.
  • ● Make-ahead tips: Prepare bulk grains and proteins at the start of the week and store them in airtight containers for up to three days. Assemble fresh bowls each day with different veggies and sauces to keep things exciting. If storing, keep wet dressings separate and toss just before eating for crisp greens and veggies.
  • ● Storage instructions: Store all components in airtight containers. Grains and proteins last 3–4 days refrigerated; veggies vary (check daily for freshness). Avocado and herbs should be added fresh.
  • ● Serving suggestions: Enjoy grain bowls cold, at room temp, or gently warmed. Pair with mason jar salads or a fruit smoothie for a complete meal.
Variations
  • ● Taco Bowl: Use rice or quinoa, top with taco-spiced chicken or beans, corn, tomatoes, guacamole, queso fresco, and salsa for flavor.
  • ● Mediterranean Bowl: Try quinoa or farro, grilled chicken, cucumbers, tomato, onion, shredded carrot, feta, and a lemon-herb vinaigrette.
  • ● Sushi Bowl: Rice with crab or salmon, avocado, cucumber, carrot, nori strips, and a drizzle of soy sauce.
  • ● Roasted Veggie Grain Bowl: Brown rice with roasted sweet potatoes, cauliflower, Brussels sprouts, chickpeas, and a creamy pesto or tahini sauce.
  • ● Breakfast Bowl: Quinoa base with breakfast sausage, fried or poached egg, and sautéed spinach.
  • ● Vegan/Vegetarian: Stick to beans, lentils, tofu, chickpeas, plant-based dressings, and pile on raw or roasted veg.
  • ● Gluten-Free: Use naturally gluten-free grains (rice, quinoa, millet) and ensure sauces/dressings are GF certified.

One-Pot Pasta Bowl

Prep: 10 minutes Cooking Time: 15–20 minutes Skill Level: Beginner Servings: 2–4 Special Equipment Needed: Large high-sided skillet or pot with lid

Ingredients

  • Aromatics & Base:
  • ● 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • ● 1 small yellow onion, finely diced (about ½ cup)
  • ● 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • ● 1 pint cherry tomatoes, halved (or 1 can diced tomatoes, drained)
  • Vegetables & Protein:
  • ● 2 cups fresh spinach or kale, roughly chopped (substitute with Swiss chard; remove tough stems)
  • ● 1 cup sliced mushrooms (optional)
  • ● 1 (15-ounce) can chickpeas, drained and rinsed (swap with white beans or cooked sausage/tofu)
  • Pasta & Liquid:
  • ● 8 ounces penne, fusilli, or spaghetti (swap for whole wheat or gluten-free if needed)
  • ● 3 cups low-sodium vegetable broth (can use chicken broth or water in a pinch)
  • Seasonings & Finishers:
  • ● 1 teaspoon kosher salt (plus more to taste)
  • ● ¼ teaspoon black pepper
  • ● 1 teaspoon dried Italian herbs (basil, oregano, thyme)
  • ● Zest and juice of 1 lemon
  • ● ¼ cup fresh basil leaves, torn (optional)
  • ● 2 tablespoons toasted pine nuts or chopped walnuts (optional, adds crunch and healthy fats)
  • ● ¼ cup grated parmesan cheese, vegan parmesan, or nutritional yeast (optional)

Instructions

  1. Heat olive oil in your pot over medium heat until shimmering.
  2. Add onion and sauté for 2–3 minutes until soft and fragrant.
  3. Stir in garlic and cook just until aromatic, 30 seconds—do not let it brown (for a deeply savory base, see tip below).
  4. Add cherry tomatoes and mushrooms. Cook for 2–3 minutes, stirring occasionally, until tomatoes start to soften and release juice.
  5. Add the chopped greens and stems (if using Swiss chard, skip kale stems as they can stay too firm). Sauté for another minute until wilted.
  6. Pour in the uncooked pasta, chickpeas, broth, Italian herbs, salt, and pepper. Give it all a good stir, making sure the pasta is mostly submerged in liquid.
  7. Bring everything to a lively simmer over medium-high heat. Reduce to medium-low, cover with a lid slightly askew, and cook for 8–12 minutes (time varies by pasta type). Stir every few minutes, scraping the bottom to prevent sticking.
  8. As the pasta absorbs liquid and begins to soften, taste for doneness. Add a splash more broth or water if things look dry before pasta is tender.
  9. Once the pasta is al dente and the sauce has thickened (most of the liquid should be absorbed, but the mix should remain creamy), remove from heat. Stir in lemon zest and juice for brightness, taste, and adjust seasoning as needed.
  10. Top immediately with fresh basil, toasted nuts, and cheese or nutritional yeast if using. Serve straight from the pot while everything is hot and fragrant.
Tips
  • ● For even deeper flavor, always sauté aromatics before adding other ingredients. Take your time—letting onions turn translucent, then adding garlic briefly ensures sweetness and avoids bitterness.
  • ● Don’t rush the pasta—you want it toothsome, not mushy. Visual cue: pasta should hold texture, and sauce should cling but not puddle at the bottom.
  • ● If all liquid is absorbed but pasta isn’t done, gradually add ¼ cup hot broth or water at a time and continue simmering.
  • ● This dish reheats well! Store leftovers in an airtight container up to 3 days. If sauce thickens, thin with a bit of water or broth when reheating.
  • ● Make-ahead tip: Chop veggies and measure spices the night before for speedy assembly after work.
Variations
  • ● Gluten-free: Use brown rice or chickpea pasta; adjust cooking time as needed.
  • ● High-protein: Stir in cooked lentils or vegan sausage during the last 5 minutes.
  • ● Creamy upgrade: Swirl in ¼ cup coconut milk or cashew cream for richer texture.
  • ● Swap in seasonal vegetables like zucchini or bell peppers. For softer textures, add at the end so they stay vibrant and lightly crisp.
  • ● Nut-free: Skip pine nuts or top with sunflower seeds for crunch.
